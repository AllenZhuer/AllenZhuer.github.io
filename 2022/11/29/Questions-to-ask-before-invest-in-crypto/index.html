Questions to ask before I invest in crypto

  1. Funding. I always look at funding first. If a project has no runway in a bear market it’s dead in the water. It’s a challenge to dig up these answers sometimes but my first steps are always Google and ask the team directly on their Telegram or Discord.
    • Do they have enough capital in their treasury for at least two years’ runway?
    • Is their treasury held in fiat, stable or their own token? Example, FTX and its token FTT.
    • Do they have access to their treasury or is it vested?
    • Do they earn fiat outside of crypto?
    • Will their revenue grow as the project scales?
  2. Team. The infomation of team members can be found in their websites, socials, twitter, and LinkedIn. And the telegram channel and discord can be a good place to ask more questions about the project and team members. Sometimes the team can make great difference on the project. The xen was very popular when it first introduced to the market and there was a gas war since the founder is the No.21 in Google.
    • Does the team have relevant experience?
    • Is their team large enough for a project of their size?
    • Is the CEO still active and does he/she appear to care?
    • Are they actively hiring?
  3. The developers in the team. Check LinkedIn for previous dev experience and @Artemis__xyz to see how active they are on GitHub. If they aren’t on Artemis you can check GitHub directly. How recent a commit is is more important than overall commit frequency though! The other reason why this is important is one of the biggest challenges in the crypto world is the code, since crypto and web3 are building on the trust and the coding is the fundemental of the safety. Hackers Already Profit Billions Dollars From Code Vulnerabilities.
    • Are the devs active? (check GitHub)
    • Do the devs have blockchain experience?
  4. Tokenomics - Basic info can be found in their whitepaper.
    • Are team and advisor wallets locked for at least 6 months?
    • Is liquidity locked for at least 2 years?
    • If the token is unreleased has it been actively traded OTC by presale investors?
    • If the token is released, is it fully distributed?
    • If not fully distributed is vesting structured to not cause massive dumps?
    • If not fully distributed what percentage of the Fully Diluted Value(FDV) has been hit and how long till 100%?
    • Is token distribution good? (i.e. team < 20%, advisors <5%)
    • Add token unlock dates to your calendar!
  5. Tokenomics - Value Accrual
    • Is there a burning mechanism?
    • Does staking provide a high yield?
    • If yield is high is there a mechanism for fighting inflation?
    • Is the locking of tokens incentivised?
    • If tokens aren’t locked are users encouraged to hold? Special function, like holding BNB can reduce middle fee when deposit and withdraw.
    • Is there an escrow mechanism to reduce inflation like GMX’s eGMX?
    • Do they pay out more than they make?
  6. Tokenomics - Investors. Check the web, whitepaper and google the VCs.
    • Are there any holders with over 5% of the supply who aren’t on the team?
    • Are you able to identify non-team big holders and do they pose a risk?
    • Are VCs invested and how long are they vested? (remember VCs can market buy)
  7. Community. Community is arguably more important than tokenomics. The value of the token is highly built on the trust. Bitcoin will be worthless if people don’t believe it. A lot of projects rally based on community alone. The only way to know if the community is strong is to spend time in their Telegram or Discord and see how good their Twitter engagement is. Examples: Shit, Doge.
    • Do they have a genuine and large social following?
    • Do they have good social media reach and engagement?
    • Does the marketing appear ethical?
    • Do they communicate with their community?
    • Do they have good and active mods on their TG/Discord?
    • Does their community appear strong and happy?
  8. Use Cases. Check their website and whitepaper for this. Use case is important for long-term growth. Bonus points if the use case is valid in a bear market.
    • Is their tech new?
    • Does their tech address successfully solve problems?
    • What is the market size for this tech and will grow?
    • Is there a desire for this tech in the space?
    • Is the tech scalable if this project were to grow?
    • Will use cases still exist in an extended bear market?
    • Are the use cases viable long-term or will they be easily replaced?
  9. Roadmap
    • Does their roadmap indicate they plan to keep building and improving?
    • Does the roadmap have realistic timeframes?
    • Add important milestones to your calendar.
  10. Users, customer segment. Do the team update info often? Is the team active in the community? Are there positive vibes on Discord? Are haters shouted down by holders?
    • Does the team care about users?
    • Are users loyal to the project?
  11. Competitors Analysis
    • Do they have a lot of competitors?
    • Do they have an edge over competitors? Unique selling points.
    • What risks do their competitors pose?
    • Do they have a stronger community than their competitors?
    • Maeket share.
    • Entry Barries.
    • Competitors’ response.
  12. Risks. A lot of these can be answered while you are doing competitor research or answering questions in other categories. Whitepapers, websites, Telegram/Discord and general knowledge of the sector are all useful for answering these questions.
    • Are there weaknesses that may prove critical?
    • Are the team focused on building or making money?
    • What are their biggest weaknesses and cant they realistically be overcome?
    • Will an extended bear market affect the project?
    • Is there a risk that regulation will kill the project? Chinese government killed the stepn project.
  13. Current Value. The fundmental analysis and technical analysis are important here. All we need to think about is when will we trade.
    • Given all of the above is their current value fair or are they undervalued?
    • If undervalued does the estimated return outweigh the potential loss?
